About Talon

Built to Win

The habit is the point

The problem is not information.

The people who download habit apps already know what they should do. They have read the books. They have watched the videos. They can explain the two-minute rule to a friend at dinner. They have started the routine, stopped the routine, and started it again.

The problem is not knowing. The problem is that nobody is designing the next small action for them, every single day, as their life actually is. The planning is what they keep failing at — not the doing.

Talon builds the habit so the person doesn’t have to plan it.

What Talon is.

Talon is a habit-building app. Not a habit tracker — the distinction matters. A tracker records what already exists. Talon generates what comes next.

The user picks a goal. Talon delivers a small, specific, finishable action. The action changes as the user progresses. There are four categories and many sub-goals inside each. There are phases the user moves through as the habit takes hold. Completed goals become planets. Sustained practice becomes a universe.

The details are in the app. The experience is the point.

The philosophy.

Most habit products are loud. They gamify, they shout, they panic the user with streak-fire emojis the moment a day slips. Talon is the opposite. Quiet. Considered. Premium.

Talon’s objective is to help the user see the paths and the actions that lead to becoming a better version of themselves. Results come from consistent, simple work — day in, day out. Talon is built to make that work visible, and to keep it in reach.

When you read something like Atomic Habits and find yourself nodding at every page but can’t make the habits stick, the problem is rarely the theory. The theory is usually fine. What’s missing is the thing that comes next — the plan that takes the theory off the page and puts it in the hand, every morning, as one small specific thing to do. Talon is built for the person who has already done the reading.

Talon does not promise outcomes. It will not say lose twenty pounds or read fifty books. It promises the habit. The doing. The outcomes are what happens to people who keep doing.

The founders.

Leo and Marcos designed this app with the intention to help people close the gap between knowing what they should do and actually doing it.

They built Talon because nothing on the market took the planning off their plates.
